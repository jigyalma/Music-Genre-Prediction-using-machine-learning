Different features used to determine the genre of the song

Popularity

This will determine the popularity of the song.

Acousticness

Songs with higher acousticness are more likely to use acoustic and non-electronic instruments. Acousticness is measured on a scale of 0 (not acoustic) to 100 (very acoustic). Acoustic and non-electronic instruments are more likely to be used in songs with a higher acousticness. The second subplot demonstrates that the majority of songs and music pieces are not acoustic.

Danceability

The danceability of a track is determined by a combination of musical elements such as tempo, rhythm, and beat. Songs with a higher danceability have more consistent and stronger beats. Danceability, like acousticness, is measured on a scale of 0 (low danceability) to 100 (high danceability) (high danceability).

Duration ms

Music duration is given in milliseconds. Most samples are not longer than 0.3e6 (300 000) milliseconds, or 300 seconds, or exactly 5 minutes. Sounds reasonable.

Energy

The perceived intensity and activity of a song is measured by energy. Energy is also measured on a scale of 0 (low energy) to 100 (high energy) (high energy). Higher energy songs are more intense, dynamic, and loud.

Instrumentalness

The presence of vocals in a track is predicted by its instrumentality. The level of instrumentality is scaled from 0 (probably contains vocal content) to 100. (likely contains no vocal content). Instrumental songs are less likely to contain vocals.

Key

In classical, Western art, and Western pop music, the key of a piece is the group of pitches, or scale, that serves as the foundation of a musical composition.

Liveness

The presence of an audience in a song is detected by liveness. The level of liveness is scaled from 0 (no audience) to 100. (audible audience). Songs with a higher liveness have a higher chance of being performed live.

Loudness

The decibel level of a song is measured by loudness. Because decibels are relative to a reference value, songs with lower loudness values are quieter than songs with higher loudness values.

Speechiness

The presence of spoken words in a song is measured by speechiness. Speechiness is measured on a scale of 0 (low speechiness) to 100 (high speechiness) (high speechiness). Songs with a higher level of speechiness are mostly made up of spoken words, similar to poetry or a talk show.

Tempo

The tempo of a song is measured in beats per minute (bpm). Many popular songs have tempos ranging from 50 to 200 beats per minute. Songs with a higher tempo move faster.

Valence

Valence assesses a song’s positivity. Valence is measured on a scale of 0 (low valence) to 100 (high valence) (high valence). Higher valence songs sound happier and more cheerful.
